Melanoma Skin Cancer

Introduction to Melanoma Skin Cancer

Melanoma is a type of skin cancer that originates in the melanocytes, the cells responsible for producing pigment. It is the most aggressive form of skin cancer and has the potential to spread to other parts of the body. Detecting melanoma early stages is crucial for successful treatment.
Melanoma is often caused by exposure to UV radiation, but genetic factors can also play a role. Individuals with fair skin, a history of severe sunburns, many moles, or a family history of melanoma are at a higher risk.
Individuals with fair or light-colored skin are at a higher risk due to reduced melanin, which provides some protection against UV radiation. A history of severe sunburns, especially during childhood, increases the risk of melanoma later in life. Having a large number of moles or unusual moles (dysplastic nevi) can be a risk factor. Regularly monitoring changes in moles is essential. A family history of melanoma or other skin cancers may contribute to an increased risk, suggesting a genetic predisposition. Individuals with weakened immune systems, whether due to medical conditions or immunosuppressive medications, may face an elevated risk. While melanoma can occur at any age, the risk increases with age, particularly in older adults. Melanoma affects both genders, but it is more common in males.

Symptoms of Melanoma

A

Asymmetry

One half of the mole is different from the other half.

B

Border Irregularity

The edges are notched, uneven, or blurred.

C

Color Changes

The color is not uniform and may include shades of brown, black, or even red.

D

Diameter

Melanomas are often larger in diameter than a pencil eraser.

E

Evolving

Any change in size, shape, color, or elevation should be monitored.


Prevention Measures
Sun Protection: Encourage the use of sunscreen with a high SPF, wearing protective clothing, and seeking shade, especially during peak sun hours.
Regular Skin Checks: Emphasize the importance of regular self-examinations and professional screenings, especially for individuals with risk factors.
Avoiding Tanning Beds: Discourage the use of tanning beds, as they significantly increase the risk of melanoma.
Educational Campaigns: Promote awareness about melanoma and its risk factors through educational campaigns, reaching out to communities, schools, and workplaces.
